# Fee Model Research — Fantasy Sports & Digital Card Trading Platforms
> Researched: 2026-03-25 | For: BeScout fee model benchmarking

---

## 1. SORARE (Fantasy + Digital Cards / NFTs)

### Revenue Streams
| Source | Fee / Take Rate | Details |
|--------|----------------|---------|
| **Primary Card Sales (Auctions)** | ~50% gross margin | Sorare mints & auctions new cards. Average sale every 3 seconds. This is the PRIMARY revenue driver. |
| **Secondary Marketplace Fee** | **5% on seller** | Deducted from seller proceeds. $100 sale = seller gets $95. Applied to Manager Sales + Direct Offers. |
| **Club/League Royalties** | 10-20% primary, 1-2% secondary | Paid FROM Sorare's margin to clubs/leagues as licensing royalties. |
| **Gas Fees** | Absorbed by Sorare | Sorare covers all Starknet/ETH transaction fees. Users pay $0 gas. |

### Key Financials
- **2022 Revenue:** EUR 143M (peak, sales volume $343M)
- **2024 Revenue:** EUR 43M (-70% from peak, sales volume collapsed)
- **2025 Forecast:** EUR 42M (flat)
- **EBITDA 2024:** -EUR 100M (halved from -200M in 2023)
- **Cash Reserves:** $230M (2023) -> $91M (2024) -> est. $41M (2025)
- **Valuation:** $4.3B (2022 peak funding round, likely marked down significantly)

### Notes for BeScout
- Sorare's model is 80%+ dependent on PRIMARY sales (minting new cards). Secondary fees are small.
- The 5% secondary fee is standard for NFT marketplaces.
- Club royalties (10-20% primary) are comparable to BeScout's IPO fee split (85% club).
- Sorare is burning cash fast — the pure NFT card model without strong fantasy engagement is struggling.

## 2. DREAM11 (India — Fantasy Sports)

### Revenue Streams
| Source | Fee / Take Rate | Details |
|--------|----------------|---------|
| **Contest Entry Rake** | **15-25%** of total prize pool | Users pay entry fee (INR 3 to INR 10,000). Dream11 keeps 15-25%, distributes 75-85% as prizes. |
| **Advertising/Sponsorships** | Undisclosed | IPL title sponsor, brand partnerships |
| **Dream11 Pass (Subscription)** | Recurring | Discounted entries, priority access, exclusive rewards |

### Prize Pool Structure
- Prize pool = Sum of all entry fees MINUS rake
- ~50% of participants win prizes (top half)
- Top-heavy distribution: #1 gets disproportionate share
- Example: 100 players x INR 100 entry = INR 10,000 total. Dream11 keeps INR 1,500-2,500. Prize pool = INR 7,500-8,500.

### Key Financials
- **FY2025 Revenue:** INR 7,370 Cr (~$880M USD)
- **FY2023 Revenue:** INR 6,384 Cr (~$770M USD)
- **FY2023 Profit:** INR 188 Cr (~$23M USD) — PROFITABLE
- **Users:** 220M+ registered
- **ARPU:** ~INR 33/user/year ($4 USD) across total base; paying user ARPU much higher
- **Status:** Paused paid contests Aug 2025 (India online gaming bill)

### Notes for BeScout
- 15-25% rake is the benchmark for fantasy sports in emerging markets.
- Dream11 proves fantasy CAN be profitable at scale with high rake.
- Subscription model (Dream11 Pass) is interesting for BeScout club subscriptions.

## 3. PRIZEPICKS / UNDERDOG FANTASY (US Daily Fantasy)

### Revenue Model — Pick'em Parlay Style
These platforms don't use traditional rake. Instead, they embed the house edge in **fixed payout multipliers**.

| Entry Type | PrizePicks Payout | Underdog Payout | Implied House Edge |
|------------|-------------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| 2-pick | 3x | 3x | ~15-20% (break-even ~57%) |
| 3-pick | 5x | 6x | PrizePicks ~20%, Underdog ~15% |
| 5-pick flex | 10-25x (tiered) | Similar | ~15-20% effective |
| 6-pick flex | Up to 25x | Similar | ~15-20% effective |

### Break-Even Win Probabilities (= House Edge Indicator)
- **2-pick:** Need ~57% win rate to break even (implied -137 odds per leg)
- **3-pick Underdog:** Need 55.03% per leg
- **5-pick flex:** Need 54.25% per leg (-118.6 implied)

### Effective Rake Equivalent
- **PrizePicks effective take rate: ~15-20%** embedded in payout structure
- **Underdog effective take rate: ~12-18%** (slightly more generous payouts)

### Key Financials — PrizePicks
- **Adj. EBITDA (12mo to Jun 2025):** $339M (60% YoY growth)
- **Revenue growth:** 60% YoY
- **Valuation:** $2.5B (Allwyn acquisition Jan 2026, 62.3% stake for $1.6B)
- **Earnout valuation:** Up to $4.15B on performance milestones
- **Available in:** 45 US states
- **Underdog valuation:** $1.23B (for comparison)

### Notes for BeScout
- Pick'em model hides the rake brilliantly — users see "payouts" not "fees."
- $339M EBITDA on a DFS platform shows massive profitability potential.
- BeScout's fantasy events could adopt similar fixed-payout structures in Phase 4.

## 4. DRAFTKINGS / FANDUEL (US Sports Betting + DFS)

### DFS Rake Structure
| Contest Type | Rake | Notes |
|-------------|------|-------|
| Head-to-Head | 7-10% | Lower rake for 1v1 |
| Small GPP (guaranteed) | 10-12% | Standard tournaments |
| Large GPP ($1 entry) | 14-16% | Mass-market contests, highest rake |
| Premium/High-stakes | 5-8% | Lower rake to attract whales |

### Key Financials — DraftKings (2024)
- **Revenue:** $4.7B (mostly sportsbook, DFS is small %)
- **Active Users:** 4.8M MUPs (monthly unique players)
- **ARPMUP:** $97/quarter (Q4 2024) — 16% decline YoY
- **Revenue per user:** ~$980/year

### Key Financials — FanDuel (2024)
- **Handle:** $50.8B wagered
- **Revenue:** $5.79B
- **Active Users:** 4.5M
- **Revenue per user:** ~$1,287/year
- **Market Share:** ~40% of US sports betting

### Notes for BeScout
- DFS rake of 10-15% is the GOLD STANDARD for traditional fantasy.
- Both companies pivoted from DFS to sportsbook — DFS alone wasn't enough.
- Revenue per user ($980-$1,287/year) shows how much engaged sports bettors spend.

## 5. NBA TOP SHOT / DAPPER LABS (Digital Collectibles)

### Fee Structure
| Source | Fee | Details |
|--------|-----|---------|
| **Pack Sales (Primary)** | 100% to Dapper (minus NBA royalty) | Dapper mints and sells packs at fixed prices ($9-$999) |
| **Marketplace Fee** | **5% on seller** | Split between Dapper and NBA (exact split undisclosed) |
| **Listing/Delisting** | Free | No fee to list or remove |
| **Withdrawal Fee** | Variable | Fees for withdrawing to external wallets |

### Key Metrics
- **Peak Monthly Volume:** $226M (Feb 2021)
- **May 2024 Volume:** $1.9M/month (99% decline from peak)
- **At peak:** $2M+/day for 52 straight days
- **Staff Cuts:** 22% (Nov 2022), 20% (Feb 2023), 12% (Jul 2023), more in 2024
- **SEC Investigation:** Closed without action (2023)
- **Lawsuit Settlement:** $4M (classified as unregistered securities)

### Notes for BeScout
- 5% marketplace fee is IDENTICAL to Sorare — this is the NFT/digital card standard.
- Primary pack sales are the real money maker, not secondary fees.
- Top Shot's collapse shows the danger of collectibles without utility (no fantasy game attached).
- BeScout's model is STRONGER because cards have trading utility + fantasy + community, not just collectible value.

## 6. FOOTBALL INDEX (Collapsed March 2021)

### Fee Structure (Pre-Collapse)
| Source | Fee | Details |
|--------|-----|---------|
| **Buy Commission** | 0% | Free to buy shares |
| **Sell Commission** | **2%** | Deducted from sale proceeds |
| **Instant Sell Spread** | **5-10%** | Selling immediately = accept lower price (spread). Market sell = 2% fee but wait for buyer. |
| **New Share Issuance** | 100% | FI minted new "shares" at will — primary revenue source |

### Dividend Structure (Unsustainable)
| Dividend Type | Payout | Funded By |
|--------------|--------|-----------|
| Match Day (single game) | 2p-12p per share | New user deposits (NOT revenue) |
| Match Day (multiple games) | 2p, 5p, 8p per share | New user deposits |
| In-Play Dividends | 1p-3p additional | New user deposits |
| Media Buzz | Based on media mentions | New user deposits |

### Why It Failed — The Economics
1. **Ponzi Structure:** Dividends were paid from new customer deposits, NOT from sustainable revenue
2. **Liabilities exceeded £1M/month** by Jan 2020
3. **Only way to sustain:** Constant new user growth + position churn
4. **2% commission was FAR too low** to cover dividend liabilities
5. **No real underlying asset** — "shares" were just bets dressed as investments
6. **Dividend slash:** Max payout cut from 33p to 6p (March 2021) — triggered bank run
7. **Customer losses:** ~£90M
8. **Regulatory failure:** UK Gambling Commission ignored warnings

### Lessons for BeScout (CRITICAL)
- **NEVER fund rewards from user deposits** — BeScout uses Treasury minting (controlled)
- **2% trading fee was insufficient** — BeScout's 6% total (3.5% platform + 1.5% PBT + 1% club) is healthier
- **"Dividend" language is dangerous** — BeScout correctly uses "Community Success Fee" and "Platform Credits"
- **Unlimited share minting** destroyed value — BeScout has 300 card cap per player + 4 tranches max
- **FI had NO utility beyond speculation** — BeScout has fantasy, community, scouting = real engagement

## 7. FANTASY PREMIER LEAGUE (Free Model)

### Revenue Model
| Source | Revenue | Details |
|--------|---------|---------|
| **Sponsorship** | Primary | Official FPL sponsors (currently undisclosed value) |
| **Brand Partnerships** | Secondary | In-app and web advertising |
| **Data/Traffic** | Indirect | 11.5M+ users driving PL website traffic, app downloads |
| **Premier League Promotion** | Strategic | FPL exists to increase fan engagement with PL matches, driving TV/streaming revenue |

### Key Point
- FPL is a **marketing tool**, not a profit center
- Zero entry fee, zero trading, zero marketplace
- Revenue comes from making fans watch MORE Premier League matches
- The PL earns ~£10B/cycle from TV rights — FPL's ROI is measured in viewership, not direct revenue

### Notes for BeScout
- Free fantasy can work IF the goal is engagement/retention, not direct revenue
- BeScout's free fantasy events serve the same purpose — drive engagement, which drives card trading
- The real money is in the card marketplace (like Sorare), not fantasy entry fees

## 8. INDUSTRY BENCHMARKS — SUMMARY

### Take Rate / Rake Comparison
| Platform | Type | Take Rate | Sustainable? |
|----------|------|-----------|-------------|
| Dream11 | Fantasy Entry | 15-25% | YES (profitable) |
| DraftKings/FanDuel | DFS Entry | 10-16% | YES (at scale) |
| PrizePicks | Pick'em Parlay | 15-20% (embedded) | YES ($339M EBITDA) |
| Sorare | Card Marketplace | 5% secondary | NO (losing money — primary sales declining) |
| NBA Top Shot | Card Marketplace | 5% secondary | NO (volume collapsed 99%) |
| Football Index | Share Trading | 2% sell commission | NO (collapsed, Ponzi) |
| **BeScout** | **Card Trading** | **6% total** | **TBD — higher than NFT standard, lower than fantasy** |

### Industry Standard Ranges
| Category | Healthy Range | Predatory | Notes |
|----------|-------------|-----------|-------|
| Fantasy Sports Rake | 10-15% | >20% | Dream11's 25% is high but accepted in India |
| Card Marketplace Fee | 5-10% | >15% | 5% is NFT standard (Sorare, Top Shot, OpenSea) |
| Sports Betting Vig | 4-10% | >12% | Sportsbooks embed in odds |
| Daily Fantasy (Pick'em) | 15-20% embedded | >25% | Hidden in payout multipliers |
| Share Trading (legacy) | 1-2% | N/A | Too low to be sustainable without other revenue |

### Revenue Per User Benchmarks
| Platform | Annual RPU | Market |
|----------|-----------|--------|
| FanDuel | ~$1,287 | US Sports Betting |
| DraftKings | ~$980 | US Sports Betting |
| Dream11 | ~$4 (all users), est. $50-100 (paying) | India Fantasy |
| PrizePicks | Est. $200-400 (implied from EBITDA) | US DFS |
| Sorare | Est. $50-150 (declining) | Global NFT Fantasy |
| Fantasy Sports Global Avg | ~$10 | Global |

### What Makes Fees "Healthy" vs "Predatory"
- **Healthy:** Fee clearly communicated, funds operations + development, user can calculate expected value
- **Predatory:** Hidden fees, misleading payout structures, rake exceeds 20% without clear value, funded by new user deposits (Ponzi)
- **Key insight:** Platforms that EMBED fees in payouts (PrizePicks) face less user resistance than those with VISIBLE fees (DraftKings rake complaints)

## 9. IMPLICATIONS FOR BESCOUT

### Current BeScout Fee Structure
| Source | Platform | PBT | Club | Total |
|--------|----------|-----|------|-------|
| Trading | 3.5% | 1.5% | 1% | **6%** |
| IPO | 10% | 5% | 85% | **100%** |
| P2P Offers | 2% | 0.5% | 0.5% | **3%** |

### Competitive Positioning
1. **Trading fee (6%)** is ABOVE the NFT card standard (5%) but BELOW fantasy rake (10-15%). This is a defensible middle ground — the extra 1% goes to clubs, which is a selling point to B2B partners.

2. **IPO split (85% club)** is extremely generous to clubs — Sorare gives only 10-20% royalties. This is BeScout's B2B advantage.

3. **P2P Offers (3%)** is competitive — lower than marketplace trading, incentivizes direct deals.

4. **No fantasy entry fee (Phase 1)** — correct strategy. Free fantasy drives engagement, trading fees are the revenue engine. Same logic as FPL.

### Risk Factors from Research
- **Sorare/Top Shot lesson:** Primary sales (IPOs) will decline over time. Secondary trading fees must sustain the platform long-term.
- **Football Index lesson:** NEVER fund rewards from user deposits. Treasury minting must be controlled.
- **Dream11 lesson:** High rake works in emerging markets IF the product is engaging enough.
- **PrizePicks lesson:** Embedding fees in payout structures reduces friction — consider for Phase 4 paid fantasy.
